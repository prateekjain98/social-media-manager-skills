## Reply Templates (Public — Value-First)

### "How do I make an infographic?"

```
A few things that make the biggest difference:
1. Start with your data hierarchy — what's the ONE number you want people to remember?
2. Less is more. If it needs more than 6 data points, it's a report, not an infographic.
3. Pick a template that matches your data type (comparison, timeline, stats, process).

Tools: Canva for general design, Piktochart for data-heavy ones.
We're also building wireframr.io — AI-powered, focused just on infographics.
```

### "What tool should I use for infographics?"

```
Depends on your needs:
- Quick social media infographic: Canva works fine
- Data-heavy business infographic: Venngage or Piktochart
- Automated from your data (no design skill): wireframr.io (we're building this — waitlist open)

Biggest question: how often do you make them? One-off = any tool. Regular = you need automation.
```

### "My report/slides look bad"

```
Three things that instantly improve any data presentation:
1. One font, two sizes (header + body). That's it.
2. Max 3 colors. Pick from your brand palette or use coolors.co.
3. Every chart needs a title that states the insight, not the category.
   Bad: "Revenue by Quarter." Good: "Revenue grew 47% YoY."

The data is probably fine. The visualization just isn't doing it justice.
```

### "Canva is frustrating" / "I hate design tools"

```
Canva does too much. It's a design tool pretending to be everything.

If you just need infographics:
- Start with the data, not the blank canvas
- Pick a template that matches your data structure
- Don't customize more than colors and text

That's the exact workflow we're automating at wireframr.io — still pre-launch
but the waitlist is open.
```

### "Best AI design tools?"

```
Depends what you're making:
- Presentations: Tome, Gamma, Beautiful.ai
- General graphics: Canva AI, Adobe Firefly
- Infographics from data: wireframr.io (we're building this — AI generates the layout from your data)
- UI/UX wireframes: Figma AI, Visily

Each tool has a sweet spot. The all-in-one tools are jack of all trades.
```
