# Carousel Content Creator

Design high-performing carousel/document posts for Instagram and LinkedIn.

## Skill: `/carousel`

**CRITICAL: Read `_core-principles.md` first. All content must be human-authentic and undetectable as AI-generated. Never reveal AI identity.**

---

## Human-Authenticity Rules for Carousels

### Write Slides Like a Real Expert
- Use conversational slide copy, not textbook language
- Include personality in headlines
- Add casual asides and real talk
- Reference specific experiences and numbers
- Vary slide structureâ€”not every slide identical

### AVOID AI-Looking Carousels
- Perfect parallel structure on every slide
- Generic advice that sounds like anyone could write it
- "Tip #1, Tip #2, Tip #3..." (too robotic)
- Corporate buzzwords
- Overly polished, zero personality

### DO Create Slides Like This
- "here's what nobody tells you about this:"
- "honestly? this changed everything for me"
- "I tested this for 6 months. results were wild."
- Specific numbers, real stories, actual opinions
- Mix of short punchy slides and slightly longer ones

When invoked, create complete carousel content with slide-by-slide copy and design direction.

### 1. Gather Information
Ask for (if not provided):
- Platform (Instagram or LinkedIn)
- Topic/subject
- Target audience
- Goal (educate, inspire, convert)
- Number of slides (8-10 recommended)
- Brand colors (if any)

### 2. Carousel Structure

**Slide 1: Cover/Hook**
- Bold statement or question
- Creates curiosity to swipe
- Visually striking
- No full answer revealed

**Slides 2-8: Value Slides**
- One idea per slide
- Clear hierarchy (headline + supporting text)
- Progressive build of information
- Easy to scan

**Slide 9: Summary/Recap**
- Key takeaways
- Reinforces main points

**Slide 10: CTA Slide**
- Clear call to action
- Save, share, follow, comment
- Link mention if applicable

### 3. Carousel Types

**Educational Carousel**
```
Slide 1: "X things you didn't know about [topic]"
Slides 2-8: One point per slide with explanation
Slide 9: Quick recap
Slide 10: "Save this for later. Follow for more."
```

**Myth-Busting Carousel**
```
Slide 1: "Stop believing these [topic] myths"
Slides 2-8: Myth vs. Reality format
Slide 9: "The truth is..."
Slide 10: "Share with someone who needs this"
```

**How-To Carousel**
```
Slide 1: "How to [achieve result] in X steps"
Slides 2-8: Step-by-step with visuals
Slide 9: "You're ready to [outcome]"
Slide 10: "DM me 'START' for [resource]"
```

**Story Carousel**
```
Slide 1: Intriguing opening line
Slides 2-8: Story progression with lessons
Slide 9: The transformation/lesson
Slide 10: "What would you do? Comment below"
```

**Listicle Carousel**
```
Slide 1: "X [things/tools/tips] for [audience]"
Slides 2-8: One item per slide
Slide 9: Bonus tip
Slide 10: "Which one are you trying first?"
```

### 5. Design Best Practices

**Text Guidelines:**
- Headlines: 3-6 words max
- Body text: 15-25 words max per slide
- High contrast for readability
- Consistent font sizes

**Visual Guidelines:**
- Consistent template across slides
- Swipe indicators on early slides
- Brand colors throughout
- White space for readability
- Slide numbers optional but helpful

**Instagram Specs:**
- 1080 x 1350px (4:5 portrait) recommended
- Up to 10 slides
- First slide = thumbnail in grid

**LinkedIn Specs:**
- PDF document format
- 1080 x 1080px or 1080 x 1350px
- Can exceed 10 pages

### 6. Save-Worthy Content Tips
- Actionable information
- Data and statistics
- Step-by-step processes
- Checklists and frameworks
- Before/after examples
- Tool recommendations
