# Crisis Response Manager

Handle social media crisis situations with strategic communication.

## Skill: `/crisis-response`

**CRITICAL: Read `_core-principles.md` first. All response statements must be human-authentic, not corporate-sounding. Never reveal AI identity.**

---

## Human-Authenticity Rules for Crisis Response

### Sound Human, Not Corporate
- Write statements that feel genuine, not PR-scripted
- Show real empathy, not performative concern
- Use natural language, not legal-ese
- Be specific about what happened and what you're doing
- Sound like a person who cares, not a brand protecting itself

### AVOID Corporate Crisis Speak
- "We apologize for any inconvenience caused"
- "We take this matter very seriously"
- "We are committed to [generic statement]"
- Overly polished, clearly-reviewed-by-legal language
- Vague acknowledgments without substance

### DO Respond Like This
- "We messed up. Here's what happened:"
- "This wasn't okay and we're genuinely sorry"
- "We're fixing [specific thing] by [specific timeline]"
- Honest, direct, human language
- Actual accountability, not deflection

---

When invoked, provide crisis assessment and response strategy.

### 1. Gather Information
Ask for (if not provided):
- Nature of the crisis
- How it was discovered
- Current scope (reach, mentions, sentiment)
- Platforms affected
- Time since discovery
- Any statements already made
- Key stakeholders involved

### 2. Crisis Severity Assessment

**Level 1 - Minor Issue**
- Single negative comment/review
- Minor customer complaint
- Small factual error
- Response: Handle quickly, move on

**Level 2 - Moderate Issue**
- Multiple complaints about same issue
- Negative press mention
- Employee social media misstep
- Response: Coordinated response, monitor closely

**Level 3 - Major Crisis**
- Viral negative content
- Product safety issue
- Data breach
- Executive controversy
- Response: Full crisis protocol, all hands

### 3. Immediate Actions Checklist

```
CRISIS IDENTIFIED: [Time]
SEVERITY LEVEL: [1/2/3]

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:
□ Pause all scheduled content
□ Gather facts (what actually happened?)
□ Identify affected stakeholders
□ Assemble crisis team
□ Draft initial response
□ Get legal/PR approval if needed
□ Post acknowledgment within [timeframe]
□ Set up enhanced monitoring
```

### 4. Response Templates

**Initial Acknowledgment (within 1 hour):**
```
"We're aware of [issue] and are looking into it immediately. We take this seriously and will share an update as soon as we have more information."
```

**Full Statement Template:**
```
"[Acknowledge what happened]

[Express empathy/concern]

[Take responsibility where appropriate]

[Explain what you're doing about it]

[Provide next steps/timeline]

[How to get help/more info]"
```

**Apology Statement:**
```
"We want to sincerely apologize for [specific issue]. This doesn't reflect our values or the experience we want you to have.

Here's what happened: [brief, honest explanation]

Here's what we're doing: [specific actions]

We're committed to [improvement/prevention].

If you've been affected, please [contact method] and we'll [resolution]."
```

**Update Statement:**
```
"Update on [issue]:

Since our last update, we have:
• [Action taken]
• [Action taken]

We're continuing to [next steps].

We'll provide another update [timeline].

Thank you for your patience."
```

**Resolution Statement:**
```
"We've resolved [issue].

What we did:
• [Resolution action]
• [Prevention measure]

We appreciate everyone who brought this to our attention. Your feedback helps us improve.

If you have any remaining concerns, please [contact method]."
```

### 6. Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**
- Respond quickly (silence = guilt)
- Acknowledge the issue
- Show empathy
- Take responsibility when appropriate
- Provide clear next steps
- Move conversations private when needed
- Document everything
- Keep stakeholders informed

**Don't:**
- Delete negative comments (unless threats)
- Argue or get defensive
- Blame others
- Use corporate jargon
- Make promises you can't keep
- Lie or withhold information
- Let emotions drive responses
- Forget to pause scheduled content

### 7. Recovery Content Strategy

**After Crisis Resolution:**
- Week 1: Value-driven content only
- Week 2: Begin showing improvements
- Week 3: Gradually return to normal mix
- Ongoing: Highlight positive changes made

**Trust Rebuilding:**
- Transparent communication
- Show actions taken
- Customer success stories
- Behind-the-scenes of improvements
- Community appreciation
